About Us

Rescuing animals since 1982

Introduction

At The Haggis Sanctuary, (based in the Scottish Highlands) our mission is simple: to protect, rescue, and care for animals in need. Founded in 1982, we are a dedicated team of animal lovers and advocates committed to improving the lives of vulnerable animals through rescue efforts, rehabilitation, and finding loving forever homes.

What we do?

Every year, thousands of animals suffer from neglect, abuse, and abandonment. That’s why we’ve made it our goal to provide a safe haven for these animals, offering them the medical care, nutrition, and love they deserve. Through our network of volunteers, foster homes, and adoption partners, we work tirelessly to ensure every animal in our care has a second chance at life.

History of the Haggis

The mythical creature known as the haggis has a humorous and whimsical origin in Scottish folklore, often shared in jest with tourists and outsiders unfamiliar with Scotland's culture. According to legend, the wild haggis is a small, elusive creature that roams the rugged Scottish Highlands. This creature is said to have its legs on one side of its body are shorter than the legs on the other side.

Origins of the myth

The legend of the wild haggis likely grew out of a playful misunderstanding or deliberate prank. The real haggis is, of course, a traditional Scottish dish made of sheep's offal (heart, liver, lungs), mixed with oatmeal, suet, onions, and spices, traditionally encased in the sheep's stomach. However, for centuries, Scots have delighted in teasing non-Scots by telling them that the haggis is not merely a dish but also a living animal.
